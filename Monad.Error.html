Error monads. This is a way to fake exceptions in pure code. Here, our computations might fail and throw some exceptional value, which can be caught in a pattern-match. Ocaml exceptions are obviously more powerful than this, but as with Monad.Option, the advantage here is that our errors are now first-class values!

Like Monad.Option, the plus operation discards the right-hand computation if neither throws an error.
